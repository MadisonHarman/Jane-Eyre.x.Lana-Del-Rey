Dark Paradise

In "Dark Paradise," Lana's experience is like a paradise at night. As opposed to being a paradise at day, where the temperature is much higher. This reveals that Lana’s memories will never be the same as actually being with him. Contrasting, on a figurative she feels it’s similar to a dark paradise, the paradise revealing that her memories of him are incredibly fond. However, its darkness suggests that Lana knows this memory is tainted, and will ultimately only bring her more pain. Lana is so desperate to have him back that she sees him whenever she closes her eyes. She can’t forget him because she sees him whenever she closes her eyes.

Every time I close my eyes

It's like a dark paradise

No one compares to you

I'm scared that you won't be waiting on the other side

This quote takes place in chapter 35, page 640, of Jane Eyre. Jane is tormented by Rochester's eerie screams and seeks him, despite not even knowing whether or not he has changed.
