The Grants

In "The Grants," Lana questions: in his utopia would she be there? Could she be his utopia? She wants to be equated to his own personal heaven. She believes she can provide him with the unparalleled bliss, pleasure and eternal happiness of heaven.

Do you think about Heaven?

Oh-oh, do you think about me?

My pastor told me when you leave, all you take

Oh-oh, is your memory

This quote takes place in chapter 35, page 639, of Jane Eyre. Jane asks Heaven to show her the right path as to whether she should marry St. John or Rochester. Of course, the answer is Rochester.
