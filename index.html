Creative Process

Whilst reading Jane Eyre, I noticed a connection between Jane Eyre & Mr. Rochester's complex relationship and underlying themes in the artist Lana Del Rey's songs. With both portraying romance as having their "ups" and "downs", I want to illuminate the overall theme that the ability to overcome one’s own virtues is a true sentiment of unwavering love through this website. I believe this medium works best because it is interactive yet informative on a topic that I thoroughly enjoy. In addition, it puts my Computer Science skills to the test!

Lana Del Rey: Identity & Purpose

The world of Lana Del Rey can be described as one compromising a desire for a beloved. Through this, “...a pantheon of enticing cultural icons arises,” especially when intertwined with her own perspective for achieving and maintaining a beloved. With this in mind, Lana seems to have "...such a total, non-moralistic, and in many ways uncompromising focus...” when it comes to desire, which is extremely important when understanding how desire fluctuates and evolves in relationships (Grande 195-196). In this case, descriptions of Lana’s “Desire for God,” “Master and Slave” dynamic with older men, and the overall intertwining of “Violence, war, and love,” can very much illustrate the progression of Jane and Rochester’s relationship, whether literally or metaphorically (Grande 199, 208, 215). Thus, these will be the aspects of Lana’s songs that we will focus on in this project.
